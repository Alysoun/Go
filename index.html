Basic Rules

  • Go is played on a grid of 9x9, 13x13, or 19x19 lines
  • Players take turns placing stones on intersections
  • Black plays first
  • Stones cannot be moved once placed
  • The goal is to surround more territory than your opponent

Capturing Stones

  • Stones must have at least one liberty (empty adjacent intersection) to survive
  • When a stone or group has no liberties, it is captured and removed
  • Captured stones count as points at the end of the game

Territory

  • Territory is empty space surrounded by your stones
  • Borders must be secure against enemy invasion
  • Each point of territory counts as one point

Scoring

  • Count your territory points
  • Add captured enemy stones
  • White receives 6.5 points compensation (komi)
  • Highest total wins

Special Rules

  • Ko Rule: Cannot immediately recapture in a way that repeats the board position
  • Suicide Rule: Cannot place a stone that would have no liberties unless it captures
  • Pass: Players can pass their turn
  • Game End: Two consecutive passes end the game
